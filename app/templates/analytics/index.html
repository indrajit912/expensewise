Understanding Your Spending Trends

This chart helps you analyze how your outlays evolve over time. By tracking patterns, you can anticipate high-spending periods and adjust your budget proactively.

 
Actual Total Spending (Purple Bars)

Represents the exact amount spent during each interval (e.g., each day, week, month, or year).

 
3-Period Moving Average (Red Dashed Line)

Calculates the average of the current and past two intervals to smooth out short-term fluctuations.

Simple Example:

Suppose you spend ₹100 in January, ₹150 in February, and ₹350 in March (e.g., due to an annual car insurance payment). Your actual March spending shows a huge spike to ₹350. However, your 3-period moving average for March calculates the average of these three periods: (₹100 + ₹150 + ₹350) / 3 = ₹200. This shows that your typical, smoothed baseline spending is ₹200, making long-term trends easier to recognize.

What to Conclude from the Trend:
  • Line is Rising: Your overall cost baseline is growing (lifestyle creep or rising costs). Consider reviewing discretionary categories to cut back.
  • Line is Falling: Your saving efforts are working! Your average consumption is contracting, leading to healthier savings.
  • Line is Flat/Stable: Your monthly budget requirements are steady and predictable, making it easy to project future expenses and automate savings.
